Monte Carlo Tree Search

Mini Project - CMSC818B

Abstract

Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) is a powerful search algorithm used in decision-making and artificial intelligence, especially in games and real-time simulations. This project focuses on implementing and improving the MCTS algorithm to demonstrate how it works, including its ability to balance exploration and exploitation during the search for optimal decisions.

The algorithm utilizes random sampling to simulate possible future outcomes and builds a decision tree based on these simulations, leading to increasingly accurate decisions over time. MCTS is highly flexible and can be applied to various complex problems such as board games, robotics, and optimization tasks.

In this project, we break down the technical aspects of Monte Carlo Tree Search in simple terms, making it accessible to individuals with varying levels of expertise in artificial intelligence.
